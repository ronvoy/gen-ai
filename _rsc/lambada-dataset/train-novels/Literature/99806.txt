 chapter 1 i do n't make friends easily , but i had come to know a delightful old woman named yvette through a mutual friend at the nursing home where they resided . 
 she died suddenly , unexpectedly -- well , unexpectedly for someone who was 86 years old . 
 and i became aware of her passing through unconventional means . 
 that day , that morning , i think is where i 'd put the bookmark in my life and say , here , this is where the path less taken most clearly presented itself . 
 were there earlier sign posts ? 
 yes , of course , and no doubt many twists still to come . 
 i was only 48 . 
 -lrb- now there 's a benefit of hanging around the nursing home : i say that without irony and with only mild regret . -rrb-

 my husband doug slumbered heavily beside me , confident that either the alarm or my insomnia would wake him and launch him into his work week .

 daniel .

 that i should keep paying attention to those little hunches , not just write them off . 
 that we all need to slow down , observe , listen , be present in our own lives .

 even the idea that it 's worthwhile to take some risks , make changes , be -- even in the tiredness of middle age -- spontaneous .
